# Research Impact Positioning

## Gap addressed

Most AI scientist publications emphasize:
- idea generation quality
- autonomous workflow breadth
- paper-writing automation

but provide limited benchmarking for:
- sequential statistical validity under adaptive stopping
- governance replayability
- adversarial robustness of scientific acceptance decisions

This repository targets that gap with an explicit benchmark for trustworthy discovery governance.
It should be interpreted as a benchmark contribution, not a claim that all protocol layers are production-complete.
It is positioned as reproducible science infrastructure for autonomous research agents.

## Communities served

1. AI scientist and agentic research communities:
- evaluate whether autonomous discovery claims survive sequential validity constraints.

2. Self-driving laboratory communities:
- stress-test update policies under adaptive experimentation and integrity threats.

3. Scientific machine learning communities:
- compare model discovery pipelines with and without audit-closed safeguards.

4. Open-science infrastructure communities:
- test transparency log and replay requirements for machine-driven science.

## Path to benchmark standardization

To position as a standard benchmark:
1. Keep protocol assumptions explicit and versioned.
2. Report confidence intervals and fixed reproducibility manifests.
3. Require deterministic replay checks for submitted methods.
4. Add plugin interfaces for external AI scientist agents.
5. Publish reference leaderboards on:
   - false discovery control
   - replication success
   - optional-stopping stability
   - adversarial resilience
